## Speaking – Rubricas (do gabarito)

The optional speaking part of the placement test is designed to be a one-to-one interview that will take up to 10 minutes. Ideally, the interviewer should be somebody who has experience speaking with non-native speakers, such as a teacher, but not somebody who has met the candidate before. The interviewer may wish to record the interview to make evaluation easier.

**Part 1**
Assign a score from 0 to 3 points as follows:
*   **3 points** if the candidate gives clear, appropriate answers to your questions **consistently**
*   **2 points** if he/she gives clear, appropriate answers to your questions **occasionally**
*   **1 point** if he/she gives clear, appropriate answers to your questions **rarely**
*   **0 points** if he/she does not complete the task at all

**Part 2**
Assign a score from 0 to 3 points as follows:
*   **3 points** if the candidate asks appropriate, grammatically correct questions **consistently**
*   **2 points** if he/she asks appropriate, grammatically correct questions **occasionally**
*   **1 point** if he/she asks appropriate, grammatically correct questions **rarely**
*   **0 points** if he/she does not complete the task at all

**Part 3**
Assign a score from 0 to 3 points as follows:
*   **3 points** if the candidate delivers a response that is **coherent, relevant, and well-developed**
*   **2 points** if he/she gives a response that is **somewhat coherent, relevant, and developed**
*   **1 point** if he/she gives a response that is **rarely coherent or relevant, or is poorly developed**
*   **0 points** if he/she does not complete the task at all

**Overall**
Get the candidate’s sub-total (out of 15) by adding up the scores for parts 1 to 5. Then determine the candidate’s final speaking score (out of 25) by adding from 0 to 10 more points as follows:
