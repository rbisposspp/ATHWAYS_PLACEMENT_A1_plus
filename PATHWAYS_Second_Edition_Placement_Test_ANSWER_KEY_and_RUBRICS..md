#### **Speaking**

The optional speaking part of the placement test is designed to be a one-to-one interview that will take up to 10 minutes. Ideally, the interviewer should be somebody who has experience speaking with non-native speakers, such as a teacher, but not somebody who has met the candidate before. The interviewer may wish to record the interview to make evaluation easier.

**Part 1**
Assign a score from 0 to 3 points as follows:
*   **3 points** if the candidate gives clear, appropriate answers to your questions **consistently**
*   **2 points** if he/she gives clear, appropriate answers to your questions **occasionally**
*   **1 point** if he/she gives clear, appropriate answers to your questions **rarely**
*   **0 points** if he/she does not complete the task at all

**Part 2**
Assign a score from 0 to 3 points as follows:
*   **3 points** if the candidate asks appropriate, grammatically correct questions **consistently**
*   **2 points** if he/she asks appropriate, grammatically correct questions **occasionally**
*   **1 point** if he/she asks appropriate, grammatically correct questions **rarely**
*   **0 points** if he/she does not complete the task at all

**Part 3**
Assign a score from 0 to 3 points as follows:
*   **3 points** if the candidate delivers a response that is **coherent, relevant, and well-developed**
*   **2 points** if he/she gives a response that is **somewhat coherent, relevant, and developed**
*   **1 point** if he/she gives a response that is **rarely coherent or relevant, or is poorly developed**
*   **0 points** if he/she does not complete the task at all

**Part 4**
Assign a score from 0 to 3 points as follows:
*   **3 points** if the candidate shows a **sustained ability to hold a natural conversation**
*   **2 points** if he/she shows an **intermittent ability to hold a natural conversation**
*   **1 point** if he/she shows the **ability to hold a natural conversation only rarely**
*   **0 points** if he/she does not complete the task at all

**Part 5**
Assign a score from 0 to 3 points as follows:
*   **3 points** if the candidate delivers a response that is **coherent, relevant, and well-developed**
*   **2 points** if he/she gives a response that is **somewhat coherent, relevant, and developed**
*   **1 point** if he/she gives a response that is **rarely coherent or relevant, or is poorly developed**
*   **0 points** if he/she does not complete the task at all

**Overall**
Get the candidate’s sub-total (out of 15) by adding up the scores for parts 1 to 5. Then determine the candidate’s final speaking score (out of 25) by adding from 0 to 10 more points as follows:

| The candidate: | |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1 Spoken Fluency** | • should speak fluently, easily, and with minimal hesitation<br>• may pause sometimes to think of ideas, but not to think of language |
| **2 Use of Grammar** | • should use a variety of grammar structures correctly<br>• should not make systematic errors when speaking |
| **3 Vocabulary Range** | • should use a variety of words naturally and accurately<br>• should show some ability to use collocation or idiomatic expressions |
| **4 Pronunciation** | • should speak at a natural speed and generally be easy to understand<br>• should make clear, natural use of stress, rhythm, and intonation |
| **5 Task Fulfillment** | • should finish each task in a reasonable time (or the given time)<br>• should give or ask for coherent, relevant, and appropriate information |

*   **2 points per criterion** if the candidate meets both of the sub-criteria **consistently**
*   **1 point per criterion** if the candidate meets one or both of the sub-criteria **intermittently**
*   **0 points per criterion** if the candidate meets one or both of the sub-criteria **only rarely**

Assign **0 points per question** if the sentence is not complete, or if the meaning of the sentence is unclear because there are too many errors or because the errors cause confusion.
